Types of Materials

Different materials require different recycling processes. Here’s a breakdown of common materials and how to recycle them:

1. Paper

Recycling paper reduces the need for deforestation and saves energy. Be sure to separate cardboard and paper from food containers.

2. Plastics

Plastics like bottles, containers, and bags can often be recycled, but be sure to check the recycling codes to see what’s accepted in your area.

3. Metals

Aluminum and steel are widely recycled, with aluminum requiring 95% less energy to recycle than to produce new. Always rinse metal containers before recycling!

4. Glass

Glass is 100% recyclable and can be recycled indefinitely. It’s typically easy to recycle, but check for local guidelines regarding color separation.

5. Electronics (E-Waste)

Items like old phones, computers, and batteries need special handling. E-waste should be dropped off at designated recycling centers, as they contain hazardous materials.
